Mini Forest - A Green Dream!

We dream to plant 1 lakh plants in the state of Telengana by end of 2022 and create a thriving and sustainable mini-forest. We plan to create livelihood projects throughout this process, right from preparing the soil for cultivation all the way to post-care activities which will last about three years.

Plantation

Mini Forest - Miyawaki Plantation Technique

Implementing agent

Natureal is going to be registered as a section 8 NGO by Feb 2022. Right now it is an association of people under the divine guidance of our guru Sri Datta Vijayananda teertha Swamiji from Avadhoota Dattapeetham which consists of lakhs of devotees who believe in sustainable development.

Beneficiaries

The primary focus of this project is to increase the green belt in places that need to enhance its greenery not just helping people through livelihood but also helping the biodiversity. The primary focus is also to plant fruit trees that can help thousands of animals throughout the state. The other focus is to create forests that can help the communities in total.

Mission

Honest, active, and deliberate youth and community engagement for tree plantation, awareness, and for supporting more livelihood projects but helping the government plant 1 lakh saplings in areas that need a green belt and support with respect to livelihood.

Measure of success

The success of our projects will be measured based on the post plantation report which would be generated with the help of EMPRI ( Environmental Management & Policy Research Institute). The number of Plantation events organized The number of awareness meetings conducted in schools and communities about plantations. The number of trees planted in the government allocated land The number of environmental volunteers formed comprised of youth for supporting the government with their future plantation and livelihood activities.

Vision

To build communities that are self-sustaining, self-reliant, poverty-free, well-informed, and environmentally conscious.

Expected output

We hope to organize multiple plantation drives throughout the state that may include both different varieties of plantation including creating a Miyawaki forest, fruit forests, and other types of high-density plantation depending on the need of the place. We will train and encourage the youth from different regions to uphold agroforestry as a way of restoring the lost forest cover. This will ensure sustained provision of products from livelihood projects without damaging the natural habitat. We hope to engage schools, colleges, youth from different communities and organize plantation drives by students to increase awareness of climate change and environmental management. This will create a generation of enlightened citizens and future leaders.

Core values

To create communities that work for both the present and future generations, we are guided by our guru who believes in natural living not just at a macro but also at a micro-level.

Impact

Increased tree cover, reduced levels of air, soil, and water pollution, improved methods of waste disposal, and reduced incidences of floods and drought. Awareness and increase in environmental volunteers from the youth

Long-term goal

Build self-sustaining, self-reliant, poverty-free, well-informed, and environmentally conscious Communities in Telangana with the help of the Government.

Project sustainability

Active engagement and involvement of youth during the training will ensure that they master the skills to sustainable living practice. These practical skills will endear in the young generation's sense of ownership for natural resources and the environment at large. The engagement of schools and communities will also ensure that the trees are nurtured and sustained. Though most importantly, the schools and communities will take ownership of the projects implemented in their jurisdiction.

Objectives

To empower the youth to create a safer, healthier, cleaner, and greener environment through the plantation drive. To alleviate poverty through the building, strengthening, and empowering the communities to create forests that will also support their livelihood.
